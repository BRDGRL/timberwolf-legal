Privacy Policy
Last updated: 2 May 2026

This Privacy Policy explains how Timberwolf Planting Pty Ltd (ABN 27 612 495 173) ("we", "us", "our") collects, uses, and protects information through the Timberwolf mobile application (the "App").

We are committed to protecting your privacy and complying with the Australian Privacy Principles (APPs) under the Privacy Act 1988 (Cth).

1. Who this policy applies to
The App is provided for use by employees and contractors of Timberwolf Planting Pty Ltd to manage tree-planting operations, record working hours, track plantings, and coordinate with crew members.

2. Information we collect
When you use the App, we may collect:

Account information — your name and email address (used to sign you in via Firebase Authentication).
Profile information — an optional profile photo you choose to upload.
Work data — hours worked, planting tallies, project assignments, expense receipts, and other operational data you enter into the App.
Device information — basic technical information such as device type, operating system version, and app version, used for troubleshooting.
Crash and diagnostic data — anonymised reports about app crashes or errors, used to improve reliability.
We do not collect precise location data, contacts, or any biometric information.

3. How we use your information
We use the information we collect to:

Provide the core features of the App (timesheets, tally tracking, project management).
Authenticate you and keep your account secure.
Sync your work data with our internal systems.
Communicate operational information with your crew and supervisors.
Diagnose and fix bugs, and improve the App over time.
4. Who we share information with
We share information only with the third-party services that make the App work:

Google Firebase (authentication, database, file storage) — operated by Google LLC.
TrackVia — our operational database for projects, tallies, and time records.
Slack — for team notifications related to operational events.
Sentry (optional) — for anonymised crash reporting, when enabled.
These providers process data on our behalf under their own security and privacy commitments. We do not sell your information to anyone.

5. Where your data is stored
Data is stored on servers operated by our third-party providers, which may be located outside Australia (including the United States and the European Union). By using the App, you consent to this transfer.

6. Data security
We use reasonable technical and organisational measures — including encryption in transit, access controls, and authenticated APIs — to protect your information. No system is perfectly secure, but we take security seriously and act promptly to address any vulnerabilities we become aware of.

7. Data retention
We keep your information for as long as your account is active and as long as we need it to run the business (for example, to keep accurate timesheet and project records). When you leave the company, your account access is removed. Historical operational records may be retained as required by Australian employment and tax law.

8. Your rights
Under Australian privacy law, you have the right to:

Request access to the personal information we hold about you.
Request that we correct information that is inaccurate or out of date.
Request that we delete your personal information, subject to legal retention requirements.
Make a complaint about how we handle your personal information.
To make any of these requests, contact us using the details below. We will respond within a reasonable timeframe (usually within 30 days).

If you are not satisfied with our response, you may lodge a complaint with the Office of the Australian Information Commissioner (OAIC) at oaic.gov.au.

9. Children
The App is not intended for use by anyone under the age of 16, and we do not knowingly collect information from children.

10. Changes to this policy
We may update this Privacy Policy from time to time. The "Last updated" date at the top will reflect the most recent revision. Continued use of the App after changes are posted constitutes acceptance of the updated policy.
